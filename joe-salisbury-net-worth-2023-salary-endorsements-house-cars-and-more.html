Joe Salisbury is a British professional tennis player who was born on April 20, 1992. Salisbury specializes in doubles and achieved a career-high doubles ranking of No. 1 on April 4, 2022. He became the third British player to acquire the world No. 1 ranking after Jamie Murray and Andy Murray (in singles or doubles).

Salisbury has won 14 ATP doubles titles till date, including six major titles. He won his third consecutive US Open title in 2023 alongside partner Rajeev Ram. In mixed doubles, Salisbury won two majors with his partner Desirae Krawczyk — the 2021 French Open and 2021 US Open.

Joe Salisbury Career

Salisbury began playing tennis at the age of three. He initially trained at the Sutton Tennis Center under Jeremy Bates. He finished his college at the University of Memphis with 97 doubles wins along with his partner David O’Hare.

Salisbury turned professional in 2014 and played his maiden ATP match at the US National Indoor Championship with O’Hare, where they were eliminated by the Bryan brothers in the first round. He won his first ATP doubles title at the Shenzhen Open along with Ben McLachlan.
