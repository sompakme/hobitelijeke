Reports have it that boxing legend Floyd Mayweather has splashed millions of dollars on a massive property along the Boulder Highway in Las Vegas.

It was gathered that the property is the iconic Crystal Palace skating rink which went on sale at the end of last year to the tune of $4.5 million.

Top outlet KSNV reports that the property which was originally built in 1986 is situated on a staggering 28,000 square-foot space.

It is said to be a piece of land that is known for first-dates, birthday parties and bringing back nostalgic memories of many Las Vegan childhoods.

So while News 3 may not have the transaction records to back it up, perhaps seeing is believing.

Meanwhile, Mayweather will finally come out of retirement to take on YouTuber Logan Paul in a bout which is slated for February 20, 2021.

The 43-year-old confirmed the bout on Social Media to announce his return to action having fought last in August 2017 against Conor McGregor to take his record to 50-0.

Mayweather was involved in an exhibition match in December 2018 against Japanese kickboxer Tenshin Nasukawa where he made $9million for just 139 seconds of work.

According to him, tickets for his fight with 25-year-old Logan Paul are already selling as both are set to make millions.

Mayweather wrote on Instagram:

"Super Exhibition Feb. 20, 2021 !!!! Early Bird Special On Sale NOW!!!! More Details Soon."

It was gathered that the early pricing is at $24.99 (£18.62) for the first million tickets sold before it rises to $39.99 (£29.80), and then to $59.99 (£44.71) starting December 29.
