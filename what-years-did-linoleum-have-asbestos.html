Asbestos was employed in the making of vinyl sheet goods up to the mid-1970s, according to historical records. It is possible that the vinyl floor in a home constructed in 1981 contains asbestos since leftover stockpiles of asbestos-containing flooring continued to be marketed until the late 1970s or early 1980s, even after its usage had been outlawed.

Vinyl Sheet Flooring Containing Asbestos. Many varieties of vinyl flooring made before to 1980 included asbestos in some form or another. After 1980, the usage of asbestos in this sort of flooring was phased out completely. If the paper-backed vinyl flooring in your house was installed before 1980, it is reasonable to presume that it contains asbestos for your own protection.
