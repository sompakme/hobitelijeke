The fuller answer is as follows: one circular mil is equal to the area of a circle with a diameter of one millimetre (milimeter) (one thousandth of an inch). The circular mil is used to denote the area of a wire having a circular cross section and is meant to be used as a reference. MCM is the older method of expressing the same thing, whereas kcmil is the modern way of expressing the same thing.
